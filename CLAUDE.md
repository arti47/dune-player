**Mandatory scope:** creation wizard (character + House) В· full in-play sheet В· native dice
engine В· assets & Determination tracking В· searchable rules library В· NPC compendium В·
Firebase multiplayer party with shared conflict tracker В· GM screen.
**Conditional resolution (from checkpoint):** solo mode **omitted** (no official rules) В·
`power-automation.js` **omitted** (powers are talents; their embedded effects live in the
roller/talent layer) В· pregens **included** (iconic characters, book-sanctioned) В·
5 expansion sourcebooks behind toggles (rules crunch only, never campaign plot).

### 1.1 Product decisions (plan review Q&A, 2026-07-06)

1. **Local-first.** Build and playtest the single-device experience first. Architecture
   stays Firebase-ready (locked schema, roles from day one), but **Phase 5 starts only
   after the First Session Playable milestone passes at a real session.**
2. **The user is a player** (not GM). Player surfaces (sheet, roller, Determination) are
   the heart; GM screen remains late and toggle-gated as planned.
3. **Dice are digital only.** Tap-to-roll; no manual physical-dice entry mode.
4. **Expansion commitment:** Sand and Dust, The Great Game, Power and Pawns =
   **committed** toggles; Masters of Dune, Fall of the Imperium = **stretch** (toggle
   architecture kept, extraction only if requested).
5. **Mixed devices** at the table вҖ” even responsive effort across the range; no form
   factor beyond the locked 360px-first baseline gets special treatment.
6. **Theme default: follow system**, toggle overrides (stored explicitly).

**Review improvements adopted:** First Session Playable milestone (after Phase 3) В·
JSON export/import backup (Phase 2) В· lifecycle events get confirmation summary +
one-step undo (Phase 4) В· persistent Momentum/Threat/Determination header on all in-play
screens (Phase 2) В· every automated rule surface links to its rules-library entry (T38
citations, Phase 3).

  House domains/roles/enemy tables go into the data files.

  adventure are excluded).

## 3. System Profile вҖ” COMPLETED (Stage A, signed off 2026-07-06)

### 3.1 Core resolution mechanic
- Skill test: roll a pool of **d20s, base 2, max 5**. Target number = **Skill + Drive**
  (each 4вҖ“8; drive must fit the motivation/statements).
- Each die вүӨ target number = **1 success**. GM sets **Difficulty 0вҖ“5** (0 Simple,
  1 Average, 2 Challenging, 3 Daunting, 4 Dire, 5 Epic) = successes required. Extra
  successes become **Momentum**.
- **Crits:** a natural **1** = 2 successes. With an applicable **Focus**, every die вүӨ
  **Skill rating** crits.
- **Complications:** a die at/above the **complication range** = a complication (negative trait,
  +50% time, or +1 Difficulty on subsequent tests). The GM sets the range per situation вҖ”
  Normal 20 В· Risky 19вҖ“20 В· Perilous 18вҖ“20 В· Precarious 17вҖ“20 В· Treacherous 16вҖ“20
  (`DATA.complications.ranges`; the roll dialog defaults to Normal, resets each roll). Buy off a
  complication for **2 Threat**. **Succeed at a cost:** a failed roll may be changed to a bare
  success (meets the Difficulty exactly, 0 Momentum) by accepting **1 complication**
  (`DATA.complications.succeedAtCost`; offered on any failed roll in the roller).
- **Buying dice (before roll, after Difficulty set, max +3):** 1st/2nd/3rd die costs
  1/2/3 **Momentum**, or generates the equivalent **Threat** instead.
- **Momentum pool:** group-shared, **max 6**, **decays 1 per scene end**. Standard spends:
  extra d20s 1/2/3 В· create/alter/remove trait 2 В· create scene asset 2 (+2 to make
  permanent) В· Obtain Information 1/question В· Keep the Initiative 2 В· extra asset move 2 В·
  asset Quality +1 for one attack 2 В· inflict lasting defeat 2 В· Resist Defeat 1 + attacking
  asset Quality.
- **Threat (GM mirror):** buy NPC dice 1/2/3 В· +1 Difficulty on a PC test = 2 (declared
  before dice bought/rolled) В· buy off NPC complication 2 В· trait 2 В· rival House enters 1 В·
  NPC Keep the Initiative 2 В· NPC "Determination" effect 3. NPC actions that would generate
  Threat for a PC instead **spend** it.
- **Determination (no push mechanic exists; this is the re-roll economy):** start each
  adventure with 1, **pool cap 3** (some talents grant more at adventure start). Earn: GM offers a point when a drive statement conflicts with the
  action вҖ” **comply** (immediate complication) or **challenge** (cross out statement, lose
  that drive's use until recovered); +1 instantly for writing a new drive statement in
  play. Spend 1 (drive statement must support the action): **auto-1** on one die before
  rolling В· **re-roll** any number of dice after rolling В· **declaration** (create/change/
  remove a trait) В· **extra action** in conflict (stacks with Keep the Initiative).
- **Traits (modifier model):** "Because of [trait], this is easier / harder / possible /
  impossible" вҖ” GM adjusts Difficulty or gates the attempt. No flat numeric modifiers.
- **Assists:** each assistant picks their own skill+drive, rolls exactly **1d20** (no
  bought dice; focuses apply), successes add to the leader's total **only if the leader
  scores вүҘ1 success** themselves.
- **Opposed tests (exact sequence):** the **defender rolls first** against their own
  target number; their successes set the active character's Difficulty (+1 per defensive
  asset in their zone, in conflicts). The active character then rolls; **equal successes =
  the active character wins**. If the active character fails, the **defender gains the
  shortfall as Momentum**, spendable immediately.
- **Extended tasks (exact procedure):** GM sets a **requirement**; each passed skill test
  scores **2 points + the Quality of an applicable asset (вүҘ1)**; Momentum can be spent to
  add points; a complication can reduce the points scored that attempt. **Multiple
  characters may contribute** tests toward the same requirement. (Powers defeat tracks
  В§3.7, recovery В§3.8, sandworm riding В§3.13 вҖ” one shared tracker component.)

### 3.2 Attributes & scales
No classic attributes. **Skills** Г—5: Battle, Communicate, Discipline, Move, Understand.
**Drives** Г—5: Duty, Faith, Justice, Power, Truth. All rated **4вҖ“8**. One generation
method: skills вҖ” archetype primary 6, secondary 5, rest 4, then **+5 free points, cap 8**;
drives вҖ” fixed array **8/7/6/5/4** ranked by importance.

### 3.3 Derived stats
Only the per-test **target number = Skill + Drive**. Nothing else is computed. Keep
`derived.js` for target-number building, asset-cap checks, and normalization/migration.

### 3.4 Skills & focuses
The 5 skills (В§3.2). **Focuses** = specializations that widen crit range to вүӨ Skill on
matching tests. Starting characters: **4 focuses вҖ” at least one on the primary skill AND at
least one on the secondary skill** (the archetype supplies those two; you may swap them but
must keep one on each). The other 2 are free. The book provides example focus lists per
skill (extract all into `data.js`).

### 3.5 Creation options
**Character wizard (8 steps, rule-legal order):**
1. **Concept** вҖ” optional faction template (Bene Gesserit Sister, Fremen, Mentat, Spacing
   Guild Agent, Suk Doctor) granting a bonus trait + **mandatory talents**.
2. **Archetype** (18: Analyst, Athlete, Commander, Courtier, Duelist, Empath, Envoy,
   Herald, Infiltrator, Messenger, Protector, Scholar, Scout, Sergeant, Spy, Steward,
   Tactician, Warrior) вҖ” grants archetype trait, sets primary/secondary skills, suggests
   focuses/talents.
3. **Skills** вҖ” 6/5/4/4/4 + 5 points (cap 8).
4. **Focuses** вҖ” pick 4 (**вүҘ1 on primary skill AND вүҘ1 on secondary skill**; other 2 free).
5. **Talents** вҖ” pick 3 (вүҘ1 archetype-related; faction templates force mandatory picks).
   Skill/drive/asset-category-bound talents (Bold, Advisor, The Reason I Fight, SpecialistвҖҰ)
   choose their parameter when picked and **may be taken again for a different parameter**
   (e.g. Bold (Battle) + Bold (Communicate) as two separate picks).
6. **Drives** вҖ” assign 8/7/6/5/4; write **3 drive statements** on the 8/7/6 drives.
7. **Assets** вҖ” pick 3, **вүҘ1 tangible**.
8. **Finishing** вҖ” 1 reputation/personality trait, **Ambition** (long-term goal tied to
   highest drive), name/appearance/relationships.

**Creation in Play (Core alternative, T40 В· `DATA.creationInPlay`):** an alternative
*creation method*, chosen **at the start of the character wizard** (Step 1 Concept offers
"Complete now" vs "Define in play"). Picking "Define in play" runs **only Concept + Archetype**,
then saves an **incomplete** character (`creationInPlay.active = true`): skills sit at the
Creation-in-Play archetype base (**primary 7 В· secondary 6** В· rest 4 вҖ” higher than planned
creation's 6/5 because there is no +5-point step; `DATA.creationInPlay.skillArray`), drives at the floor 4, a fixed faction's
mandatory talent + trait are granted up front (a choose-one faction's talent is picked when
defining talents in play). The player then *defines* their remaining choices during play from
the live sheet's tracker вҖ” a set of limited-use options (trait Г—1, skills Г—3 at 4/5/6,
focuses Г—2, talents Г—2, drives Г—4, ambition Г—1, assets Г—2). Defining a drive statement grants
1 Determination immediately; a drive can't be challenged until the character is complete;
completing every option unlocks advancement. (The rules-library "Creating a character in play"
card is the reference.) **Not** a post-creation toggle on a finished sheet вҖ” completed
characters never show the tracker.

### 3.6 Conditions & statuses
No fixed condition list. Complications create **negative traits** (injured, exhaustedвҖҰ)
that **auto-apply**: +1 Difficulty on affected tests or gate actions impossible. Removal:
**2 Threat** buy-off В· ally describes a fix + **Difficulty 2 test** В· specific talents
(e.g. Adrenaline Shot suppresses for a scene; Rapid Recovery removes injury for 2 Threat).

### 3.7 Health, damage & death
**No HP, no soak.** Attacks are contested tests; each defensive asset in the defender's
zone = **+1 Difficulty** to the attacker (armor also raises the wearer's movement
complication range by its rating). Minor NPCs lose = defeated instantly. Notable/Major
characters and PCs have an **extended-task defeat track**: requirement вүҲ defender's
relevant skill + defensive asset Quality; each winning attack scores **2 + asset Quality**.
**Defeat вү  death** (out of scene: yielded, unconscious, disgracedвҖҰ). **Lasting defeat**
(attacker spends 2 Momentum after defeat) = death/dismemberment/ruin per conflict type.
**Resist Defeat** вҖ” once per scene, cost 1 Momentum (or Threat) + attacker asset Quality,
suffer a complication, stay in the scene.
**Guided defeat procedure UI:** defeated вҶ’ offer Resist Defeat (if unused this scene) вҶ’
attacker lasting-defeat choice вҶ’ ally stabilize **Difficulty 2** (prevents permanent
effect; character still out for the scene) вҶ’ record permanent negative traits if unstopped.

### 3.8 Rest & recovery
- **Non-lasting defeat:** ally runs an extended task, requirement **4 + Quality** of the
  defeating asset; success = rejoin scene.
- **Lasting defeat:** ally **Difficulty 2** stabilize; survivor cannot rejoin the scene.
- **Challenged drive statements:** recover at end of any scene of contemplation (if no
  Determination spent/gained) or automatically between adventures вҖ” either write a **new
  statement**, or **вҲ’1 the challenged drive / +1 next-lowest** (keep the statement if the
  drive stays вүҘ6; below 6 the statement is lost). Enforce these limits.

### 3.9 Powers вҖ” RESOLVED: no separate subsystem
Voice, Mentat computation, prana-bindu, prescience etc. are **talents** with embedded
mechanics, resolved in the roller: e.g. **Voice** вҖ” spend up to 3 Threat for auto-successes
(1/point) on an influence Communicate test or to resist the Voice; **Mentat Discipline** вҖ”
one d20 auto-counts as a 1 on data-related Understand tests; **Calculated Prediction**,
**Prana-Bindu Conditioning** (re-roll a die on body-control Move/Discipline tests). Every
talent with a dice effect must be automated ("tap to use"), not just displayed.

### 3.10 Advancement
**Advancement points:** ambition progress **1 minor / 3 major** В· defeated in conflict
**1** В· fail a Difficulty вүҘ3 test **1** В· GM spends вүҘ4 Threat at once **1** В· group award
**1 max/session**. **Max one advance purchased per adventure.** Costs: **skill +1** (cap 8)
= 10 + 1 per previous skill advance В· **new focus** (skill must be вүҘ6) = # focuses owned В·
**new talent** = 3 Г— # talents owned В· **asset вҶ’ permanent** = 3 В· **asset Quality +1** =
3 Г— current Quality. **Drives never advance by points** (narrative challenge/recovery
only, В§3.8). **Retraining:** halve an advance's cost (round up) by permanently dropping a
skill point (min 4), focus, or talent. The app automates earning, spending, and the
one-advance-per-adventure gate.

### 3.11 Inventory & wealth
Abstract **assets** (tangible or intangible; Quality ratings; created-in-play assets are
**Quality 0** unless a printed value exists вҖ” checkpoint ruling #3). **Max 5 permanent
assets** per character (the **Specialist** talent raises the cap) вҖ” enforce, don't just
warn. No encumbrance, no coin-counting; currency (solari) is narrative. Purchases dip into
the **House Wealth index** (e.g. noble ubiquities 0, advanced tech +1, personal
ornithopter +4 вҖ” extract the full price ladder).

### 3.12 Combat structure
One engine, **5 conflict types**: Dueling, Skirmish, Warfare, Espionage, Intrigue вҖ”
differing in scale, valid assets, and the skills/drives used. **Zones** may be physical or
abstract (social circles). Turn structure: GM picks the first actor (or spends 2 Threat to
seize it); after each turn initiative passes to an opposing side; **Keep the Initiative** =
2 Momentum/Threat, ally acts at +1 Difficulty, never twice in a row; last actor of a round
picks (or pays 2 to keep) the next round's opener. **One action per turn** (move self/asset
1 zone, attack, create trait/asset, etc.); 2 Momentum = extra zone or second asset move.
**Subtle move** (Diff 2: no reactions, Keep the Initiative costs 0) / **bold move** (Diff
2: force an enemy asset to move 1 zone). Attacks: declare asset + target вҶ’ pick
skill+drive per conflict type вҶ’ **defender rolls first**, successes (+1 per defensive
asset in their zone) = attacker's Difficulty вҶ’ attacker rolls, **tie = attacker wins**,
failed attack banks the shortfall as defender Momentum вҶ’ resolve per В§3.7. **Architect/Agent duality:** Architect-scale actions use
assets-in-play (often gating possibility, not bonuses); actions on behalf of the House use
**House skill + personal drive** as the target number (GM assigns House drive if no
character leads).

### 3.13 Bestiary & NPCs

1 focus; 0вҖ“1 talents) В· **Notable** (role + optional reputation trait; drives 7/6 rest 5,
1 statement; skills 7/6/5/5/4; focuses on skills вүҘ6; 1вҖ“2 talents) В· **Major** (2вҖ“3 traits;
full drives 4вҖ“8 with statements on вүҘ6; skills all 4 + 11 points, cap 8; 3вҖ“5 focuses; 2вҖ“4
talents or NPC special abilities). NPCs run on **Threat** (3 Threat = Determination
effect). Extract **all ~25 generic archetypes** (Arrakeen Native вҶ’ Water Seller) and

### 3.17 Scene & adventure lifecycle вҖ” the app owns these events
A **scene** = one place/time/cast. **End of scene** (GM button; local-mode button):
**вҲ’1 Momentum** from the pool В· **temporary assets expire** (unless 2 Momentum spent to
make permanent) В· **Resist Defeat flags reset** В· prompt **challenged-statement recovery**
(if the character reflected and no Determination was spent/gained that scene). Traits do
not auto-expire вҖ” they end when no longer true (manual removal; single-test complications
auto-clear).
**Start of adventure:** Determination resets to **1** (+ talent bonuses, cap 3);
`advancesPurchasedThisAdventure` resets. **End of adventure:** purchase **max 1 advance**;
unrecovered challenged statements **auto-recover** (player picks new-statement or вҲ’1/+1
per В§3.8).

### 3.18 Supporting characters вҖ” the second player-controlled character type
Alongside main PCs, players share a pool of **supporting characters** (`DATA.supportingCharacters`,
T40) вҖ” the House's lesser servants (functionaries, spies, soldiers), created as needed and
**not permanently owned** by any one player. At scene start a player picks which of their
available characters to control; the controlled character is a full PC for that scene.
**Two types:** **Minor** (1 job trait В· single Drive 4вҖ“8 [typ 5 В· secondary domain 6 В·
primary domain 7 В· low-rank 4] В· skills 6/5/5/4/4 В· focuses 1@6/2@7/3@8 В· House-granted
talent only В· costs **1 Momentum or +1 Threat**, one free when you control no other character
in the scene В· unlimited) and **Notable** (1 role trait, **+1 cost** for a reputation trait В·
drives 7&6 rest 5, 1 statement, **+1 cost** for a 2nd В· skills 7/6/5/5/4, **+1 cost** for +1 to
two В· focuses 1@6/2@7/3@8, **+1 cost** for 2 more В· 1 talent, **+2 cost** for a 2nd В· costs
**3 Momentum or +3 Threat** plus extras, reuse = **half the creation cost, rounded up** В·
**max 5 per adventure**, House may adjust).
**Uncontrolled characters** (a player's other characters in the same scene) may only:
attempt **Difficulty-0 tests** (higher auto-fails), **assist**, **follow orders** (with the
orderer assisting), be **sacrificed** (1 Momentum / +1 Threat to take a controlled character's
fate), or **count as a trait** (make a multi-person task possible / lower Difficulty, down to 0
in concert). If your controlled character is defeated, immediately **take control** of an
uncontrolled one. *(These are player-side rules; the GM's NPC tier recipes are T27 in
`data-npcs.js`.)*

## 13. Onboarding & Tutorial вҖ” design decisions (Q&A sign-off 2026-07-11)

The Phase 7 tutorial (В§9) is a **guided sample session** teaching a total beginner вҖ” someone
who knows neither Dune's 2d20 rules nor the app вҖ” to play, learn-by-doing. Design fixed by
one-question-at-a-time review:

1. **Form: guided sample session** (scripted learn-by-doing), not coach-marks / read-only pages / ambient hints.
2. **Structure: modular lesson menu** вҖ” short (~3вҖ“5 min) self-contained lessons picked from a menu, progress-tracked (done checkmarks).
3. **Data: hybrid** вҖ” teaching lessons run on a **disposable sandbox** (demo character + demo pools, never persisted); the **"Create your character"** lesson **hands off to the real wizard** so the learner graduates owning a real character.
4. **Placement: first-launch prompt + Settings/About entry only** вҖ” no bottom-nav tab, no Home card (minimal footprint).
5. **v1 = 6 lessons (full core):** Your first test В· Momentum/Threat/Determination В· Drives & statements В· Conflict & defeat В· Create your character В· Scene lifecycle & advancement. The menu is extensible.
6. **Mechanism: self-contained lesson widgets** вҖ” purpose-built demo controls the script drives (isolated from the real screens), reusing the real engine (`evaluateDice`, attack math) so what's taught matches what's played. (Exception: 7e opens the real wizard.)
7. **Dice: scripted outcomes** вҖ” demo rolls use predetermined dice so each lesson reliably shows its concept (a guaranteed crit, a guaranteed complication, enough successes for Momentum).
8. **Demo character: learner picks a pregen** вҖ” choose one of the 12 iconics at the start; the pick seeds the lessons (scripted regardless) and the graduation build.

Constraints carried in: all lesson text **paraphrased** from `DATA` (В§12, no copied rules prose;
app conveniences labeled); every teaching surface `cite()`-links into the rules library; full
a11y (В§5); 0px overflow at 360px; teaching lessons must not mutate real characters/pools/House
(only 7e writes real data, via the wizard).
